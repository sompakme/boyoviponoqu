Taylor Swift Changes Song Lyrics For Travis Kelce As They Consider Marriage

September 2024 · 4 minute read

Highlights

  • Taylor Swift gave a sweet shout-out to Travis Kelce during her concert in Buenos Aires, changing the lyrics of her song "Karma" to reference him instead of her ex-boyfriend Joe Alwyn.
  • Reports suggest Taylor and Travis are thinking about marriage and kids, with popular gossip site Deux Moi claiming that they will get married and have children.
  • Taylor's previous relationship with Joe ended because he didn't want to get married, as hinted in her song "You're Losing Me," which fans believe references Joe's reluctance to take the next step in their relationship. There are concerns about Travis's intentions, with Selena Gomez being suspicious and his ex-girlfriend issuing a warning to Taylor.

Taylor Swift proved she’s head-over-heels once again for Travis Kelce when she gave him a sweet shout-out during a recent concert by changing her song lyrics.

While performing in Buenos Aires, Argentine on Saturday, November 11, the singer made a subtle switch-up during her song “Karma.” Cameras caught Travis blushing and flashing a big smile when Taylor sang, “Karma is the guy on the Chiefs, coming straight home to me.”

The original lyrics are supposed to read, “Karma is the guy on the screen, coming straight home to me,” which was previously believed to be a reference to Taylor’s long-time boyfriend, actor Joe Alwyn, who she split with earlier this year after six years together.

Though Taylor and Travis haven’t been shy to flaunt their relationship in public, the two haven’t made a lot of comments about their high-profile relationship. Joe And Taylor Reportedly Broke Up Because He Didn’t Want To Get Married

When Taylor split with Joe Alwyn, reports suggested it was because the British actor was hesitant to tie the knot with the pop star. Fans went wild in May when Taylor released her new song “You’re Losing Me,” which many believe hints at Joe’s reluctance to go to the next step in his relationship with Taylor.

“You say, ‘I don’t understand,’ and I say, ‘I know you don’t,” Taylor sings in the song. “We thought a cure would come through in time; now, I fear it won’t.”
