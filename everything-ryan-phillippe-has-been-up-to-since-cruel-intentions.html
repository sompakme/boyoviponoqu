Everything Ryan Phillippe Has Been Up To Since 'Cruel Intentions'

August 2024 · 4 minute read

Back in the 90s, a fresh crop of young performers managed to rise to fame and usher in a new era of teen idols. Movies like Scream and I Know What You Did Last Summer played a big part in this, as did Cruel Intentions, which featured performers like Sarah Michelle Gellar, Reese Witherspoon, and Ryan Phillippe.

Phillippe had multiple hits in the 90s that put him on the map, and it seemed like the actor was going to be an unstoppable force on the big screen. While he didn't have a career like Tom Cruise, Phillippe has remained busy since the 90s and has done and achieved far more than some people would suspect.

So, what has Ryan Phillippe been up to since Cruel Intentions? Let's take a closer look at the work that he has done since that movie helped make him a star.

Phillippe Became A Film Star In The 90s

As one of the major young stars who rose to prominence during the latter part of the 90s, Ryan Phillippe was seemingly unstoppable during that era. The actor was able to get several years of experience prior to breaking out, but once he was given the chance to shine, he broke into the mainstream and never looked back.

1997's I Know What You Did Last Summer was a huge box office hit that, along with Scream, helped breathe new life into the horror genre, specifically slasher films. Scream may have gotten more critical acclaim, but I Know What You Did Last Summer generated over $125 million at the box office, and its financial success wound up spawning an entire franchise of films.

Two years later, in 1999, Phillippe starred in Cruel Intentions with a bevy of young stars, much like he had in I Know What You Did Last Summer. The film might have gotten mixed reviews from critics, but after making over $70 million at the box office against a small budget, Phillippe had another sizable hit on his hands. Much like I Know What You Did Last Summer, this film caught on with younger audiences and became a staple of the decade in no time at all.

After breaking out in the 90s and becoming a star, Phillippe has remained quite busy on both the big and small screen.

He's Appeared In Movies Like 'Crash'

Two major hits got the ball rolling for Phillippe, and as the new millennium rolled around, the actor looked to capitalize on his newfound fame. 2001's Gosford Park was a modest hit for Phillippe, and after a string of smaller projects, the actor found himself in a critical darling after taking part in 2004's Crash. Phillippe, along with the rest of the cast, won a SAG Award for his performance in the film, and the picture itself won the Academy Award for Best Picture.

From that point forward, Phillippe would continue to act in projects of all sizes. He appeared in movies like Flags of our Fathers, Stop-Loss, The Lincoln Lawyer, and more. It has been a mixed bag of success, but Phillippe has maintained steady work on the big screen, constantly showing what he can do while the cameras are rolling.

As nice as it has been for fans to see Phillippe in action on the big screen, the actor has made sure to do plenty of television work, as well.

He's Been On Shows Like 'Shooter'

Before breaking out as a film star, Phillippe had done plenty of television work early on in his career. So, it shouldn't be too surprising to see that he has done a load of television work since the 90s.

Through the years, Phillippe has been on shows like Chicago Hope, The Outer Limits, Damages, Drunk History, Brooklyn Nine-Nine, and Will & Grace. That is an impressive list of credits, but these have been guest spots. Phillippe, however, has made sure to star on his own shows.

from 2016 to 2018, Phillippe starred on the series Shooter, which was based on the Mark Wahlberg film of the same name. The show was able to last for 31 episodes over the course of 3 seasons, and Phillippe did a solid job as the lead on the series.
