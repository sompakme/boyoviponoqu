Charlotte Flair and Rockford Collection have teamed up for a new line of unisex rings, revealing the collection with an announcement on July 11.

In total, six “Eminence” rings are featured on the company’s website and they retail from $1,840-$3,510. Each ring features different customization options including stones and type of metal.

Flair commented on the new line of jewelry, noting that she was proud to reveal a collaboration that was months in the making.

Charlotte Flair has been absent from WWE programming since losing an “I Quit” match to Ronda Rousey at WrestleMania Backlash in May. WWE claimed Flair would be out indefinitely due to suffering a fractured radius during the match. Since then, she got married to Andrade El Idolo and fans are eagerly awaiting her return to television.

During the build to WrestleMania Backlash, Flair previewed the bout and stated why she doesn’t have to prove anything to Rousey.

“I have nothing left to prove,” said Flair. “I already beat Ronda on the grandest stage. I was the first female to beat her in a singles competition.

“The Queen” then compared her WrestleMania triumph over Rousey to her other accolades and noted that this one was particularly meaningful because it solidified her spot at the top of the card.

“It’s hard, they’re all so different,” said Flair. “I mean beating Asuka’s streak at WrestleMania 34 was a huge accomplishment. Main-eventing WrestleMania 35, but I think beating Ronda just cemented that I’m the top woman here. So every accomplishment has a different feel to it.”
