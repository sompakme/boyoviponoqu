Earnings From Albums Sales and Tours

Quavo’s record sales as a member of Migos and a solo artist have netted him millions of dollars. 

300 Entertainment and Quality Control Music previously managed Migos. The band, however, left 300 Entertainment in 2015.

Under 300 Entertainment, the band only made $30,000-$40,000 each performance, while with Quality Control Music, they garnered up to $60,000.

Migos earned $150,000 for each show on their 2016 tour.

Likewise, their 93-show tour earned them a stunning $25 million between 2017 and 2018, with Quavo receiving one-third of the proceeds.

Migos is a highest-selling trio in the music business, with 4 billion online streams as one of the most prominent streaming groups on internet platforms.

Earning From Brand Endorsements and Collaborations

As a member of Migos, Quavo is associated with numerous businesses, including Beats By Dr. Dre, Mountain Dew, Avianne Jewelers, and Finish Line.

Quavo, as a solo artist, has begun to accept personal brand sponsorships in recent years.

When his friend and rapper Meek Mill became a co-owner of the retail athletic firm Lids in 2019, Quavo became the company’s brand ambassador.

Lids is North America’s largest retailer of headgear and licensed sports merchandise.

The rapper also partnered to launch a limited-edition hat collection, which netted him additional money.

From gaining recognition and boosting his net worth through music to being the face of retail goods and earning a second income, Quavo has created a name for himself as a great artist and savvy brand advocate.
